Welcome to the Study Techniques Library!

Discover proven study strategies that can help you learn more effectively, remember information longer, and improve your confidence. The most successful students combine several techniques rather than relying on only one method.

🧠 Active Recall

What is it?

Active Recall is a learning technique where you try to remember information without looking at your notes. Instead of rereading, you force your brain to retrieve information from memory.

How do I use it?

  • Read a section of notes.
  • Close your notes.
  • Write down everything you remember.
  • Check your notes for anything you missed.
  • Repeat regularly.

Why does it work?

Retrieving information strengthens memory pathways in the brain. The more often you recall information, the easier it becomes to remember during assessments.

⏰ Spaced Repetition

What is it?

Spaced Repetition involves reviewing information multiple times over increasing periods of time rather than cramming everything into one session.

How do I use it?

  • Study a topic today.
  • Review tomorrow.
  • Review again after three days.
  • Review after one week.
  • Review after two weeks.

Why does it work?

Reviewing information before you completely forget it helps move knowledge into long-term memory and improves retention.

🍅 Pomodoro Technique

What is it?

The Pomodoro Technique is a time-management strategy that breaks study into short periods of focused work followed by short breaks.

How do I use it?

  • Study for 25 minutes.
  • Take a 5-minute break.
  • Repeat four times.
  • Take a longer break of 15–30 minutes.

Why does it work?

Short study sessions help maintain concentration and prevent mental fatigue.

👨‍🏫 Feynman Technique

What is it?

The Feynman Technique involves explaining a concept using simple language as if you were teaching it to someone younger.

How do I use it?

  • Choose a topic.
  • Explain it in simple words.
  • Identify parts you cannot explain.
  • Study those areas again.
  • Repeat until the explanation is clear.

Why does it work?

Teaching requires deep understanding and quickly reveals gaps in your knowledge.

🔀 Interleaving

What is it?

Interleaving means mixing different topics or subjects during one study session instead of focusing on only one topic.

How do I use it?

  • Study Topic A.
  • Switch to Topic B.
  • Move to Topic C.
  • Return to Topic A later.

Why does it work?

It improves your ability to choose the correct strategy when solving different types of problems.

🗺️ Mind Mapping

What is it?

Mind maps are visual diagrams that show how ideas and concepts connect to each other.

How do I use it?

  • Place the main topic in the centre.
  • Create branches for key ideas.
  • Add supporting details.
  • Use colours and images.

Why does it work?

Mind maps help organise information and improve understanding of relationships between concepts.

🃏 Flashcards

What is it?

Flashcards are study cards that contain questions on one side and answers on the other.

How do I use it?

  • Create questions from your notes.
  • Test yourself regularly.
  • Separate cards into easy and difficult groups.
  • Review difficult cards more often.

Why does it work?

Flashcards combine Active Recall and Spaced Repetition, making them one of the most effective study tools.

✍️ Blurting

What is it?

Blurting involves writing everything you remember about a topic onto a blank page from memory.

How do I use it?

  • Study a topic.
  • Put your notes away.
  • Write everything you remember.
  • Compare your answers with your notes.
  • Review what you missed.

Why does it work?

It highlights knowledge gaps and strengthens recall.

📓 Cornell Notes

What is it?

Cornell Notes is a structured note-taking system that divides a page into notes, cues, and summary sections.

How do I use it?

  • Take notes during learning.
  • Write key questions in the cue column.
  • Summarise the page at the bottom.

Why does it work?

It improves organisation and makes revision easier.

🎨 Dual Coding

What is it?

Dual Coding combines words and visuals to help your brain process information through multiple pathways.

How do I use it?

  • Add diagrams to notes.
  • Use timelines and flowcharts.
  • Include labelled images.
  • Connect visuals to written explanations.

Why does it work?

Using both visual and verbal information improves understanding and memory.

📋 Practice Testing

What is it?

Practice Testing involves answering questions, quizzes, and past papers before an assessment.

How do I use it?

  • Find practice questions or past papers.
  • Answer questions without looking at notes.
  • Mark your answers honestly.
  • Review mistakes.
  • Repeat regularly.

Why does it work?

Practice testing strengthens memory, improves exam confidence, and identifies weak areas.

❓ Elaboration

What is it?

Elaboration involves asking questions and connecting new knowledge with what you already know.

How do I use it?

  • Ask "Why?" questions.
  • Ask "How?" questions.
  • Look for real-life examples.
  • Connect ideas together.

Why does it work?

Thinking deeply about information improves understanding and memory.

🧩 Chunking

What is it?

Chunking breaks large amounts of information into smaller, manageable groups.

How do I use it?

  • Group related information together.
  • Create categories.
  • Learn one chunk at a time.
  • Combine chunks later.

Why does it work?

The brain remembers smaller groups more easily than long lists of facts.

🎯 Goal Setting

What is it?

Goal Setting means creating clear objectives for each study session.

How do I use it?

  • Set specific goals.
  • Make goals realistic.
  • Track progress.
  • Celebrate achievements.

Why does it work?

Goals provide motivation, direction and focus.

📅 Study Scheduling

What is it?

Study Scheduling involves planning your study sessions ahead of time.

How do I use it?

  • Create a timetable.
  • Assign subjects to different days.
  • Schedule breaks.
  • Review and adjust regularly.

Why does it work?

Planning reduces stress and prevents last-minute cramming.

🔍 Error Analysis

What is it?

Error Analysis means reviewing mistakes to understand why they happened.

How do I use it?

  • Review incorrect answers.
  • Identify mistakes.
  • Write corrections.
  • Practise similar questions.

Why does it work?

Learning from mistakes helps prevent repeating them.

🗣️ Teaching Others

What is it?

Teaching Others involves explaining concepts to another person.

How do I use it?

  • Choose a topic.
  • Explain it clearly.
  • Answer questions.
  • Identify knowledge gaps.

Why does it work?

Teaching reinforces understanding and strengthens memory.

⏱️ Exam Simulation

What is it?

Exam Simulation involves practising under real exam conditions.

How do I use it?

  • Set a timer.
  • Work without notes.
  • Complete the assessment.
  • Mark your work.

Why does it work?

It improves confidence, timing and exam readiness.

📖 Reflection Journal

What is it?

A Reflection Journal records your study progress and areas for improvement.

How do I use it?

  • Write after studying.
  • Record successes.
  • Identify challenges.
  • Plan improvements.

Why does it work?

Reflection helps improve study habits over time.

🎵 Mnemonics

What is it?

Mnemonics use acronyms, rhymes or memorable phrases to help remember information.

How do I use it?

  • Create acronyms.
  • Use rhymes.
  • Create memorable phrases.
  • Connect ideas to images.

Why does it work?

The brain remembers meaningful patterns more easily than isolated facts.
